About Me

My journey into engineering started in music studios, assisting Grammy-winning producer Joe West with equipment setups and recording sessions. That hands-on problem-solving extended into theater, where I led technical teams and performed lead roles in over a dozen productions.

Today, I'm a Computer Engineering student at the University of Alabama, driven by a deep curiosity and love for building. I serve as President of ABXY (UA Gaming Club), Vice President of Quad Squad Volleyball, and co-founded Alabama Competitive Programming.

I’m currently building full-stack applications, embedded systems with STM32, and game engines in C++. I am starting an embedded systems research position at the University in the Fall of 2025. With a recent victory at the University of Alabama Innovate Hackathon, I plan to pursue further hackathons with my roommates! No matter what I do, I thrive on the process of improvement.
